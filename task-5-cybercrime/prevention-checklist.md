# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

> Cyber threats are rising fast across India. This checklist gives you simple,
> actionable steps to protect yourself online — your identity, your money, and your data.

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
  Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager
  (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) so you never reuse passwords.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
  Turn on 2FA for email, social media, and banking apps. Prefer an authenticator
  app (like Google Authenticator) over SMS-based OTPs when possible.

- [ ] **3. Never click unknown links in messages, emails, or WhatsApp.**
  Verify the sender before clicking any link. Phishing links often mimic
  college portals, scholarship sites, or food delivery platforms.

- [ ] **4. Download apps only from official sources.**
  Use the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Never install APKs sent via
  WhatsApp or Telegram — they are a common malware delivery method.

- [ ] **5. Review app permissions before granting access.**
  A flashlight app does not need your contacts or location. Deny unnecessary
  permissions and regularly audit installed apps on your phone.

- [ ] **6. Use a VPN on public/college Wi-Fi networks.**
  Public Wi-Fi in canteens, libraries, and hostels is often unsecured. A VPN
  encrypts your connection and prevents data interception.

- [ ] **7. Log out of shared devices and clear browser history.**
  Always log out from college lab computers, library systems, or any shared
  device after use. Never save passwords in shared browsers.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (High Priority)

- [ ] **8. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — ever.**
  Legitimate banks, UPI apps (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), and customer support
  agents will **never** ask for your PIN or OTP. Sharing it under any pretext
  is the single most common cause of UPI fraud.

- [ ] **9. Be alert to "collect request" scams on UPI.**
  Scammers send fake UPI collect/payment requests disguised as refunds or
  scholarships. Remember: **entering your PIN to receive money is always a
  scam.** You only enter your PIN to *send* money, never to receive it.

- [ ] **10. Set transaction limits on your UPI and banking apps.**
  Most banking apps let you cap daily transfer limits. Set a low default limit
  and raise it only when needed to minimise losses if your account is compromised.

- [ ] **11. Verify QR codes before scanning at shops or events.**
  Fraudsters paste fake QR codes over legitimate ones. Always confirm the
  payee name shown in the app before authorising any payment.

---

## 🔐 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **12. Keep your social media profiles private.**
  Avoid publicly posting your phone number, college roll number, home address,
  or daily schedule — these details are exploited in targeted scams and stalking.

- [ ] **13. Think before you post — digital footprints are permanent.**
  Screenshots, reposts, and cached pages mean nothing truly disappears online.
  Avoid sharing content that could be used for identity theft or harassment.

---

## 🚨 Reporting a Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know is a victim of online fraud, cyberbullying, identity
theft, or any other cybercrime, report it immediately through these official channels:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (toll-free, available 24×7) |
| 🏛️ **Local Cyber Police Cell** | Visit your nearest cyber police station |
| 📱 **Bank Fraud Helpline** | Call your bank immediately to freeze the account |

> ⚠️ **Time is critical in financial fraud.** Report to your bank AND call **1930**
> within the first hour of a UPI/banking fraud to maximise the chance of fund recovery.
