# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
**Author:** Yajat Kataria  
**Role:** Student Digital Ambassador  

As a student at VIT Bhopal, your digital footprint is your permanent resume. Use this checklist to stay professional and safe online.

## The Do's (Best Practices)
* [cite_start]**Verify Before Sharing:** Always fact-check news or academic notifications before reposting to avoid spreading misinformation[cite: 68].
* [cite_start]**Enable Privacy Settings:** Regularly audit your account settings to control who can see your personal photos and posts[cite: 68].
* [cite_start]**Network Professionally:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni, professors, and industry leaders[cite: 31, 68].
* [cite_start]**Think Before You Post:** Ask yourself if a future employer would be comfortable seeing the content you are about to share[cite: 68].
* [cite_start]**Credit Original Sources:** If you share someone else's work, research, or art, always provide proper attribution[cite: 68, 102].

## The Don'ts (Risky Behaviors)
* [cite_start]**Don't Over-share Personal Data:** Never post sensitive info like your registration number, home address, or phone number publicly[cite: 38, 68].
* [cite_start]**Don't Engage in Cyberbullying:** Avoid toxic online arguments or sharing content intended to harass others[cite: 68, 77].
* [cite_start]**Don't Post in the Heat of the Moment:** Avoid posting angry or emotional responses that could damage your professional reputation[cite: 68, 71].
* [cite_start]**Don't Share Real-time Location:** For your personal safety, wait until you have left a venue before posting "at the location" photos[cite: 68].
* [cite_start]**Don't Use Unprofessional Handles:** Avoid using offensive or immature usernames; stick to variations of your real name for professional accounts[cite: 39, 68].
