In this module, I analyzed how poor digital communication can lead to significant academic setbacks. 
I studied a scenario where a student sent an informal email to a professor requesting a deadline extension. 
The email lacked a clear subject line, used "text-speak" like "plz help," and failed to include the student's 
registration number. Consequently, the professor ignored the email, and the student received a zero for the assignment.

This situation could have been avoided by applying basic email etiquette: using a formal salutation,
a descriptive subject line, and a polite, structured body. Through this task, I learned that digital 
communication is a direct reflection of one's professionalism. As a Student Digital Ambassador, I will 
teach my peers that a well-formatted email is not just about politeness, but about ensuring their message
is actually read and respected by the recipient.
