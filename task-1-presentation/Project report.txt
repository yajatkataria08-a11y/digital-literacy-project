To fulfill my first assignment as a Student Digital Ambassador, I created a "Digital Literacy Guide for Students" using Canva.
I chose this tool because of its professional templates and intuitive drag-and-drop interface, which allowed me to focus on content 
hierarchy and visual clarity without needing advanced graphic design skills.The design is organized into five distinct sections
to provide a comprehensive overview for my peers. It defines digital literacy through critical thinking and content creation,
outlines essential digital tools like Google Workspace, and provides actionable advice for maintaining a professional online presence. 
Furthermore, it covers critical safe internet practices, such as two-factor authentication, and concludes with a guide to professional
email etiquette.One thing I found particularly interesting during the creation process was the challenge of "minimalism" in digital communication
. I realized that while there is a vast amount of information regarding cybersecurity and professional branding, the most effective way to reach
my batchmates is through short, high-impact bullet points. Distilling complex topics like phishing and digital citizenship into a single-page 
resource helped me better understand how to communicate technical advice responsibly and effectively.
